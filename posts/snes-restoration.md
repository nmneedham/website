Well, first blog post. Im currently working on restoring my 1993~ SNES or (Super Nintendo Entertainment System) for the non-gamers, but first, the funniest story of how I found it.

## The background story

It was a couple weeks into June and my Wife's younger brother was having a graduation party from High School, and this party was near my old house in northern Illinois. My dad was cleaning up some things from the old house, and I had asked him if he could retrieve my brothers XBOX 360 from the house because ive been on a retro-ish kick.

He called me when my wife and I arrived at the party to come pick up the "Xbox" and he left it outside, and also told me it was in bad shape.

Pictured below is the "xbox"

![Definitely an xbox](/assets/Image/IMG_5402.jpg)

## The teardown

I started by taking off the case as recommended by the reddit post I had made, [Reddit Post](https://www.reddit.com/r/snes/s/1amaoZghVh), and had given it a deep clean with alcohol 99% and baking soda. Under the hood, it was pretty rusty and corroded.

![Case off of SNES](/assets/Image/IMG_5435.jpg)

Next I inspected the screws to the motherboard and determined they were all rusted. WD40 worked in this spot. The only one I couldnt get was to the voltage regulator so I desoldered the 3 feet attached to the board. I was able to remove all screws that needed to be removed so I can extract the board. One of them broke, oopsies.

Since this console was outside in the backyard shed for close to 25 years, I didnt bother checking the capacitors and other components and ordered a capacitor set on Ebay. Upon further inspection, I will have to remove the rusted channel switch, and either find a replacement, or remove the piece completely.

I will make another blog post once I get all the parts received. Below is the power regulator, and the detached motherboard.
